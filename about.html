About Us

Baringo County Students Association is committed to fostering a strong sense of unity among students from Baringo County, regardless of their backgrounds. We believe in inclusivity and equality in all student activities.

2024

Founding and Formation

BUSCA was established by a group of visionary university and college students from Baringo County who recognized the need to bring together learners from diverse institutions under a common umbrella. The founding of BUSCA aimed to:

  • 1. Promote academic excellence
  • 2. Address common student challenges
  • 3. Foster leadership development
  • 4. Unite students through county identity and culture
  • 5. Serve as a bridge between students and the government

The early meetings were informal, held during school holidays and county events, but eventually, a formal structure, constitution, and leadership were developed.

2024-2025

Growth and Expansion

Over time, BUSCA expanded its reach across Kenya by incorporating students from public and private universities, TVET institutions, colleges, and other tertiary institutions. The association began to:

  • 1. Host Annual General Meetings (AGMs) bringing together students from across the country
  • 2. Conduct mentorship programs and high school outreach
  • 3. Organize career fairs, cultural events, and county development forums
  • 4. Participate in policy advocacy and youth representation at the county level

The creation of zonal or institutional representatives improved coordination and ensured students in various regions remained connected to BUSCA programs.

2025

Recent Developments

BUSCA has taken bold steps in recent years to strengthen internal leadership, reform governance structures, and enhance impact. Under various dynamic presidents, the association has:

  • 1. Undertaken leadership trainings and exposure tours for student leaders
  • 2. Engaged in climate action and anti-drug campaigns
  • 3. Reformed committee structures, including replacing treasurer roles with publicity managers for better communication
  • 4. Focused on accountability, performance-based leadership, and strategic partnerships
  • 5. Pioneered the Baringo County Students Day as a flagship end-of-year AGM and celebration
